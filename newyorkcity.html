This series of maps visualizes the footprint of an aspect of the city on the world and vice versa.

A city's residents are a large part of our perception of a city, whether they are our older or newest neighbors. The information they carry are made visible in these maps, allowing us to ask questions of the much larger system of a city through this small piece of data on its newer residents. This visualization shows the job title, the origin country, and the economic sector of the permanent visa applications for companies in New York City in the years 2012 and 2013.

Permanent visas, also known as permanent labor certification, is issued by the Department of Labor and is an employeement visa that is extended to foreign workers indefinitely. Permanent visas are categorized by US economic sector from "Hospitality" to "Homeland Security". Each of these sectors reflects differently on the world map, allowing the user to see patterns in country-specific professional sectors.
